Examination Timetabling System

Advanced optimization algorithms for creating efficient examination schedules at Zambia University College of Technology IT Department

Research Project Overview

This project implements and compares three optimization approaches for solving the examination timetabling problem: Genetic Algorithms, Simulated Annealing, and a novel Hybrid approach combining both methods.

Genetic Algorithm

Evolutionary approach using selection, crossover, and mutation operators to explore the solution space and find optimal examination schedules.

Simulated Annealing

Probabilistic optimization that escapes local optima by accepting worse solutions with decreasing probability as the temperature parameter cools.

Hybrid Optimization

Novel approach combining GA for broad exploration and SA for local refinement, achieving superior results through intelligent algorithm integration.

Research Problem

Examination timetabling is a well-known NP-hard constraint satisfaction problem that educational institutions face during academic planning.

  • Student Conflicts: No student can take multiple exams simultaneously
  • Room Constraints: Capacity limits and facility requirements
  • Time Constraints: Exam duration, breaks, and faculty availability
  • Preference Balancing: Multiple conflicting objectives to optimize
